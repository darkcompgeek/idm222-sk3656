Awbury Arboretum Rebranding

For my branding class, I worked with Awbury Arboretum, a historic house and natural park, to develop a new brand identity for them.` This gave me valuable experience in working with a client.

Logo

Awbury wanted their brand to emphasize nature and community, so I carefully developed the new mark to bring out these qualities. The intertwined trees highlight Awbury’s efforts to reach out to the local community. The bare tree branch emphasizes the wild, imperfect, nature of Awbury.

Awbury System

I created a new branding system for Awbury Arboretum, incorporating Typography, Colors, and Image-Making. I used this system to create a map, transit poster, website design, and Instagram ad.

Transit Poster

I hand painted this illustration on an iPad Pro with Apple Pencil using the Adobe Fresco app. I wanted to make an illustration that had a lush, inviting feel while having a painted, “secret garden” vibe.

Welcome Sign

Signage was one of the big issues that Awbury wanted to fix. Their existing signage was hard to read, and people often drove past Awbury because their welcome sign didn’t stand out at all.

I 3D modeled this welcome sign using Blender and textured it using Adobe Dimension.

Secondary Signage

I created a secondary system of signage as well. This system is designed to be clean and easily readable.

Map

I hand painted this illustration of Awbury’s Francis Cope House. This scene is the first sight you see as you drive into Awbury, and I wanted to use that as a pleasant introduction to the Arboretum.

I painted Awbury’s Map based off satellite imagery of the Arboretum. I incorporated vector elements like dotted lines and icons so that the essential information clearly stands out.
